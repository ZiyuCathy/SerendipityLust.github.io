  1. Duration and volatility
    Calculate a bond’s average maturity by its duration.
    Duration is the weighted average of the times when the bond’s cash payment are received.
    The times are the futures years 1, 2, 3, etc., extending to the final maturity date.
    The weight for each year is the present value of the cash flow received at that time divided by the total present value of the bond.
    Duration measures the exposure of the bond’s price to fluctuations in interest rates. It is how bond prices change when interest rates change. For this purpose, it is best to use modified duration or volatility. (Investors regularly monitor the duration of their bond portfolios to ensure that they are not running undue risk.)

  • measures the percentage change in bond price for a percentage-point change in yield.
